One of the first questions I get from clients is: *"Should we use WordPress or Shopify?"* The honest answer is — it depends on what you're selling and how you want to run it.

## Go with WordPress (WooCommerce) if…

- You want **full control** over design, content and structure.
- Content and SEO matter as much as sales — blogs, landing pages, resources.
- You're comfortable with a slightly larger toolbox in exchange for flexibility.
- Budget matters: WordPress is typically the **most affordable** build.

## Go with Shopify if…

- E-commerce is the **whole point** — you live in orders, inventory and checkout.
- You want payments, shipping and tax handled out of the box.
- You'd rather not think about hosting or security patches.

## My rule of thumb

If the store *is* the business, Shopify's guardrails save you time. If the website is a marketing engine that also sells, WordPress gives you room to grow. Either way, I'll set up hosting, SEO and analytics so it launches ready to perform.
